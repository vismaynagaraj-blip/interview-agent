HR Guidelines & Recruitment Portal

Professional HR guidelines sourced from recruitment portals and hiring standards

Interview Do's
  • Research the company's mission, culture, and recent news
  • Prepare 5-7 STAR stories that cover multiple competencies
  • Arrive 10 minutes early (or join video call 3 min early)
  • Bring copies of your resume and a notepad
  • Maintain eye contact and positive body language
  • Ask thoughtful, prepared questions at the end
  • Send a thank-you note within 24 hours
Interview Don'ts
  • Never speak negatively about past employers
  • Avoid vague answers — always use specific examples
  • Don't discuss salary until the employer raises it
  • Avoid interrupting the interviewer
  • Don't exaggerate or fabricate experience
  • Never ask about time off in the first interview
  • Avoid checking your phone during the interview
STAR Framework
S
Situation — Set the context. Where were you, what was the challenge?
T
Task — Your responsibility. What were YOU specifically tasked with?
A
Action — Steps you took. Use "I" not "we". Be specific about YOUR role.
R
Result — Quantify outcomes. %, $, time saved, users impacted.

Protected Topics — Never Asked in a Legal Interview

By law, interviewers cannot ask about: Age, Race/Ethnicity, Religion, National Origin, Disability Status, Marital/Family Status, Pregnancy, Sexual Orientation, or Genetic Information. If asked, you have the right to decline or redirect the answer.

Professional Network Strategy

Build your professional network to unlock hidden job opportunities

LinkedIn Optimisation
  • Headline: Use keywords: "Senior SWE | Python | AWS | 2x Startup Founder"
  • About: Lead with your strongest achievement in 3 sentences
  • Featured: Pin your best project, article, or media
  • Post weekly — consistency builds algorithmic reach
  • Request 3 recommendations before applying
  • Use Open to Work (private to recruiters only)
Informational Interviews
  • Identify 3-5 people in your target role/company
  • Send personalised connection requests (not generic)
  • Request a 20-min coffee chat, not a job
  • Prepare 5 thoughtful questions about their journey
  • Follow up with value: share an article they'd find useful
  • 80% of jobs are filled through networking, not job boards
Build Your Digital Brand
  • GitHub: Pin 3-5 projects with clear READMEs and live demos
  • Portfolio: Deploy a personal site with case studies
  • Writing: Publish technical posts on Medium or Dev.to
  • Contribute to open-source in your target domain
  • Speak at meetups or host workshops
  • Recruiters check GitHub before interviews — keep it clean
LinkedIn Cold Message Template

Hi [Name],

I came across your profile and was genuinely impressed by your work at [Company], particularly [specific project/achievement].

No worries if you're busy — and thank you either way for the inspiration!
